How to Use This Tool

A step-by-step guide for electrical technicians, engineers and contractors.
Follow the tabs from left to right for a complete calculation workflow.

Taewoo Digital Techniques LLC — Basic Edition
1

📋 Project Tab

Start here every time. Fill in the project name, client, location, and engineer name. These details appear on your final report. Set your supply voltage (e.g. 415V for three-phase Nigeria), frequency (50Hz), and default power factor (0.8 is standard).

💡 Tip: Setting the right voltage here affects ALL calculations throughout the tool automatically.
2

📝 Loads Tab

Enter every electrical load in the building. Use a preset (e.g. "Office Floor") to get started quickly, then add or remove rows to match your actual loads. For each load enter: description, category, quantity, unit wattage, power factor, and demand factor.

💡 Tip: Demand Factor is how often a load runs at full power. Lighting = 1.0. Sockets = 0.4. HVAC = 0.8–0.9.
3

📊 Demand Tab

Select your building type to apply the correct demand factor, or enter a custom value. The tool calculates your Connected Load, Demand Load, kW, kVA and a Design kVA with a growth margin (default 20%) already added.

💡 Tip: Always use the Design kVA figure (with growth margin) when sizing your main equipment.
4

⚡ Current Tab

Click "Import kW from Demand" to pull in your demand figure automatically. The tool calculates your line current using the correct formula for your system (3-phase or single-phase). A 125% design factor is applied to give you the safe sizing current.

💡 Tip: Always use Design Current (×1.25) — not the line current — when sizing cables and breakers.
5

〰 Cables Tab

Click "Import Design Current" to auto-fill from the Current tab. Select your cable material (copper/aluminium), insulation (XLPE/PVC), and how the cable will be installed (conduit, tray, clipped etc.). The tool recommends the correct IEC cable size with full derating applied.

💡 Tip: Ambient temperature and number of grouped circuits both reduce cable capacity — the tool handles this automatically.
6

🔌 Breakers Tab

Click "Import from Current Module" to fill in the full load current. Select the load type — motor and continuous loads require a 125% rating rule. The tool recommends the correct MCB, MCCB or ACB from standard IEC ratings.

💡 Tip: For motor loads, always select "Motor Load" type and add a DOL or Star-Delta starter in your panel design.
7

📄 Report Tab

Click "Generate Report" to produce a full professional summary of your project. Then click "Print / Save PDF" and choose "Save as PDF" in your browser. The report includes your project details, full load schedule, and all equipment recommendations.

💡 Tip: In the print dialog, enable "Background graphics" for the best-looking PDF output.

✅ Recommended Workflow

  • Always start at the Project tab and set your system voltage correctly
  • Use the load presets as a starting point, then customise for your project
  • Work through the tabs from left to right — each tab feeds into the next
  • Use the "Import" buttons to carry results between modules automatically
  • Always use the 125% Design Current for cable and breaker sizing, not the raw line current
  • Generate and save your report as a PDF before closing the browser
  • For official project submissions, have the report reviewed and stamped by a registered engineer
